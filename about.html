Who we Are

Dr. Muhammad Al-Faqih Hospital was established, integrated and sophisticated in its construction and equipment to keep pace with the development of health services in the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia and the world and to attract high medical competencies in various medical specialties, hoping that we will be part of the health progress in our dear kingdom and contribute to the achievement of Vision 2030.

We strive to provide health services to meet the aspirations of our patients and their families, with concern, respect, sympathy, and give them hope, and to present the patient's need over others, and to work as a team that complements each other with high professionalism and make our efforts to reach the best services and obtain the best results.

We were keen to implement an advanced and modern electronic information system to achieve a speedy delivery of patient service upon review, limit the use of papers and save all information electronically, making it easier to access by the patient and the doctor.

Community prevention of diseases is one of our main goals, and as a continuation of the path of Dr. Muhammad Al-Faqih, the Community Health Research Center for Preventive Studies was established as part of the main activities of the hospital. About it research published in reliable international medical journals such as the British Lancet magazine.

The design of the hospital building

The design of the hospital building reflects our interest in the heritage of our country, while using the latest approved construction methods to provide comfortable places, facilitate the movement of patients, control infection and conserve energy.

The hospital's location was carefully chosen in the city of Riyadh on the eastern part of King Abdullah Road near its intersection with Salman Al Farsi Road at the metro station, to make it easy to reach from any area in the city of Riyadh.

Word of the Chairman of the Board of Directors

Dr. Mohammed bin Rashid Al-Faqih is considered one of the achievements of the scholarship education system in the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia.

We mention some aspects of his medical history here.

After returning from specialization in heart surgery in Britain and America, he supervised the establishment of the heart department at the Military Hospital in Riyadh, which started with only ten beds, and performed the first operation for open heart surgery in April 1979.

The department, under his leadership, supported the state in all stages of its development until it became one of the major centers in the world in this specialty with a capacity of more than 170 beds, and was honored in the name of Prince Sultan bin Abdulaziz, may God have mercy on him.

During his surgical career, Dr. Muhammad Al-Faqih performed more than six thousand cardiac surgeries for adults, children and newborns, and he also performed heart surgeries in other countries commissioned by the state. He is considered the first to introduce cardiac surgery for newborn infants and heart transplant (transplant) in the Arab region.

King Fahd bin Abdulaziz, may God have mercy on him, honored him with the King Abdul Aziz Medal and the Order of Merit of the first degree.

And the kings and presidents of the Gulf Cooperation Council honored him with the Medal of Science.

Harvard University, known in America, honored him by inviting him as a visiting professor in cardiac surgery, and Lomalanda University in America honored him with a human service certificate, and he was appointed as a non-resident professor of heart and lung sciences and he is a clinical professor of surgery at King Saud University. Afex honored him in recognition of his important views in developing a device for the surgical treatment of atrial fibrillation in the heart.

The British Royal College of Surgeons in Edinburgh appointed him as an examiner in it and honored him by placing his name on one of its chairs, and he was elected vice-president of the World Society of Cardiothoracic and Vascular Surgery at one of its international conferences in London.

The Arab Thought Association honored him as a pioneer of the heart in the Arab world, and the Dubai government honored him with the Hamdan Award for Distinguished Medical Personalities, and was honored by the Saudi, Egyptian, Iraqi, Bahraini and Gulf Heart Associations and the Saudi Society for Surgery, and Al-Faisal University in Riyadh. The Saudi Heart Association created an award for distinguished research in heart diseases and surgery, named after him.

An attribute known to everyone who contributed to their treatment, which is sympathy with the patient and his family, and even in his dealings with medical students whose teaching or training contributed. An attribute that we hope will be reflected in the treatment of everyone who works in this hospital, and that it will remain basically constant for work in it.
